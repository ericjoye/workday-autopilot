Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before you install.

Does it really work on Workday?
Yes — that's all we do. Workday Autopilot was built from the ground up to handle Workday's unique form structure, including multi-page applications, dynamic fields, conditional sections, and their quirky validation rules. It's not a generic autofill tool that sort of works on Workday. It's purpose-built.
Is it safe? Where does my data go?
Your data never leaves your browser. Everything — your resume, contact info, work history — is stored locally using Chrome's built-in storage. We don't have servers. We don't collect data. We can't see your information even if we wanted to.
What about other job sites like LinkedIn or Indeed?
Workday Autopilot is focused exclusively on Workday. That's where the biggest pain is, and that's where generic tools fail the most. If you need autofill for other sites, tools like LinkedIn's built-in Easy Apply or generic form fillers work fine there. We're the specialist for the platform that needs it most.
How does auto-submit actually work?
Once you've entered your profile, you navigate to any Workday application and click the Autopilot button in your toolbar. The extension fills every field it can identify, then automatically clicks Submit. You can review everything before it submits, or enable "full auto" mode to skip the review step entirely.
How do I upgrade from Free to Pro?
Click "Go Pro" in the extension popup or on this page. You'll be prompted to enter payment details and choose between monthly ($14.99/mo) or yearly ($99/yr) billing. Your profile data carries over instantly — no re-setup needed.
